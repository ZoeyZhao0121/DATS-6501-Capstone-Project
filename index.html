Introduction / Background


The U.S. commercial airline industry plays a significant role in global economics and development. It is also a key element to US economic recovery after dismal pandemic years. By visualizing the key metrics and indexes from 1995 to 2020 and forecasting further development, this paper attempts to overview this vital and competitive airline industry, show the fundamental elements to success and identify important trends to provide insightful observations for better facing the opportunities, risks and challenges.


Problem Statement


The U.S. commercial airline industry is a major component of the global economy, and will continue to grow with the demand for high-speed transportation individuals. This research aims at analyzing and comparing 3 different types of airlines based on various metrics and indexes related to revenue, expense, operation, and employment, organized by airline type, operating region or aircraft size to present an overview of individual evolution, important trends and economic loss from 1995 to 2020 as well as providing insightful observations and solid prediction for better facing the recovery post-COVID. The project focuses on visualizing long-standing business approaches, identifying emerging trends and predicting recovery models of the ultra-low-cost carriers(ULCCs), which is an emerging airline business model in the U.S. and now represent almost 7% of all commercial departing seats, by comparing with network airline carriers and value carriers, which are extremely capital and labor intensive and have been keeping the winning streak and outpacing the economic growth over the past two decades.
